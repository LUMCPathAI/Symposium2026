About the Group

The Department of Pathology at LUMC advances computational pathology across research and clinical practice. We work at the intersection of pathology, imaging, and data science—developing robust data pipelines, modern machine learning models, and translational applications to improve diagnostics and patient care.

This symposium brings together staff from the LUMC Pathology Department, LKEB, CAIRELab, Biomedical Data Sciences, and invited colleagues from LIACS, Technical Medicine, Medicine, and Biomedical Sciences, as well as collaborators from other UMCs.

What is Computational Pathology?

Computational pathology applies algorithms to whole-slide images (WSIs), histology patches, and multimodal data. Common approaches include multiple instance learning (MIL), feature extraction with modern backbones, and downstream tasks such as subtype classification, survival analysis, and molecular prediction.

  • Data & Labels: from slide-level outcomes to pixel/region annotations.
  • Scalable Pipelines: acquisition, QC, de-identification, storage, & governance.
  • Modeling: patching, MIL pooling, self-supervised features, and robust evaluation.
  • Clinical Integration: deployment, monitoring, and human-centered implementation.
